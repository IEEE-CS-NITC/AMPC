Anand Memorial Project Competition

Introduction

Tathva'18 proudly presents Anand Memorial Project Competition, exclusively for the first years of NITC.

The latest entrant to the grand list of events, AMPC is conducted in the fond memory of Anand, from Electrical and Electronics Engineering department, who unfortunately passed away last year.

The top ten abstracts shortlisted, will be funded and technically supported in all aspects, to make plans and ideas, a reality.

Here's a golden opportunity to showcase your technical wizardry on a whole new level. So what are you waiting for? Dash through the rules and get started!

Rules

Rule 1

Submission of project ideas should be done exclusively by first years.

Rule 2

Teams are expected to have minimum of 3, and maximum 5 members.

Rule 3

Members of a team need not be from the same branch.

Rule 4

One person should exclusively belong to just one group.

Rule 5

Candidates can submit ideas for their projects with a brief description about their endeavor.

Rule 6

Teams are welcome to submit more than one abstract for their projects.

Rule 7

Interdisciplinary projects are highly encouraged. The project can be related to computer science, electronics, mechanical, etc. or a combination of any branch.

Rule 8

Once selected, students will be provided with access to labs and other facilities. The projects will also receive funding upto 5k as well as mentorship.

Tips

Tip 1

Students can refer projects online at websites such as

  • Hackaday
  • Hackster
  • Instructables

Although students can draw inspiration from these plagarism is strictly prohibited.

Tip 2

In case of doubts regarding project ideas, send in your queries to ampc@tathva.org.

FAQs

Who are all eligible?

All first year B.Tech students are welcome to participate.

Whom to contact in case of doubts?

All doubts and queries are to be directed to ampc@tathva.org along with your group name.

What are the facilities provided in the lab?

Specified facilities will be made available to students depending on the project selected.

What are the deadlines for registration and submission of abstract and projects?

Last date of registration and submission of abstract is on August 30th*.

Final submission of the project is on October 10th*.

*subject to changes.

What is the format for submission?

The abstract should be a pdf document, in "Times New Roman" font, size 12 and should consist of a project abstract, technical details and estimate.

Abstract Example

What is the maximum funding available?

Funding will be available upto ₹5000.

What is Mentorship?

Once a group is selected, they will be assigned two seniors who will guide them throughout their project.

What is the word limit for the abstract?

The abstract should have a maximum of 500 words.
